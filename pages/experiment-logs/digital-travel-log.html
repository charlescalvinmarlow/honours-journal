Digital Travel Log

return

Aims
  • to see roughly where I send and retrieve data from in an average day. (non-comprehensive)
  • to experiment with Data Visualisation
  • to practice designing on paper
Precedents / Context
  • Web Size Extension
  • Book As Object: wtf is a JPEG
Process / Methods
  • Take note of the different services, apps, websites that I visit and use across a day
  • I did this on Google Keep on my phone, however I kind of gave up around 10:30 having already visited many sites
  • Figure out how to get some idea of where those sites live physically
    • Geo IP can give rough estimates of where an IP address lives
    • To get an IP based of my physical location, in terminal you can use: nslookup (url)
    • then search the IP addresses returned by nslookup on ipgeolocation.io
    • find the location listed on the geoip and research the distance from Sydney
  • Create a visualisation
    • I tried to start with paper and sketch out some ideas, it was helpful to get ideas out, but I couldn't judge the quality of an idea in these lo-fi sketches
    • So I jumped in to just try making something, at first I tried to make a digital looking visualisation with rings around the user (me) but it ended up looking like a drone strike map
    • So I tried again, paring it back and focusing on what I want the data to say, using a more bar graph style
Reflection on Action

I learnt a lot during the research phase of this experiment, where servers are, who controls them, how much information is publicly available, how these things work (not fully though, god it's complicated).

However, the visualisation part of the experiment was not so fruitful, I found it quite difficult to even get to an idea of how it should look, drawing out ideas on paper but not feeling like they really helped to show whether they work or not. When I gave up and just decided to make something, to experiment, I made the wrong thing. I know this is a step that must be taken to make the right thing, but it still feels kind of bad when you look at it and know it is the wrong thing. So I stopped working on that and tried a different approach, which was definitely a step in the right direction, but the visualisation of information is still a challenge for me.

Reflection for Action
  • Display the data in a table so it can be processed into what is important and other ways it might be expressed
  • If repeated I could use browser history for a comprehensive list of sites visited on a certain day
  • Could be combined with the browser extension experiment to create a live database/visualisation
  • Research how people prototype on paper
  • Research data vis as practice
  • How do people track their travel logs in the real world? Where else could inspiration be found for this?
