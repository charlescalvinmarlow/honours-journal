Something Material Honours Journal

AGNSW Library Artist Books visit

return to Week 3

I didn't really know what to expect with this outing, I wasn't familiar with the Library, or Artist Books as a medium really so I really just went for the ride to see what happened.

What I found interesting about the books themselves, was their sense of play, and for the most part their crafty imperfection. They felt like they had stories that you only get a glimpse of by actually holding the book.

Listening to the stories of the Artists, and the way the Library operates and acquires these books was also interesting to listen to. I asked about how they might acquire more today, if it is going to art fairs etc, but since many are donated, a question that comes to me now is; If I made an Artist Book and donated it, would they keep it?

I also talked to Claire briefly about accessing the collection, it is a kind of tease to see a small sample of a hidden collection, and she mentioned that while they can be searched online there aren't really any pictures for most of them. She talked a little about how you digitise a book, do you video leafing through it etc. I was asking these questions out of a curiosity of what is in the collection and how to explore it, but I realised later that perhaps it is an opportunity out in the real world to make something, so I contacted Zoe to see what might come of offering to make a sample digitised archive.

The space was also nice and could be a nice place to work and find inspiration amongst the many books.
